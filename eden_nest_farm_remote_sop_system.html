Morning block · 5:30 AM – 9:00 AM IST
5:30 AM
Open farm & walk premises
Visual check for overnight damage: predator entry, equipment failure, unusual bird behaviour
5:45 AM
Water system inspection
Test all nipple drinkers; confirm tank is full; note and fix any blockages before feeding
Record: tank level, any blocked lines
6:00 AM
First feeding round
Distribute layer feed at standard quantity per shed. Do not skip or reduce without supervisor approval.
Record: kg of feed given per section
7:00 AM
First egg collection & grading
Collect shed by shed. Grade into: A = clean & sellable · B = hairline crack, grade 2 sale · C = broken, unusable. Store in labelled trays.
Record: count by grade (A / B / C) per shed
7:20 AM
Dead bird removal & recording
Remove any deaths immediately. Note count and likely cause (injury, illness, heat stress). Alert supervisor if more than 3 in one morning.
Record: count + cause in daily logbook
7:30 AM
Send morning WhatsApp report to Roshan
Use the morning template exactly — eggs AM, feed given, deaths, health status, notes. Send by 7:30 AM IST every day without exception.
8:00 AM
Ventilation & heat management check
Kerala heat builds fast from 8 AM. Open vents, check exhaust fans. Birds showing open-beak panting = urgent — alert supervisor.
8:30 AM
Medication or supplement water (if scheduled)
Check the weekly schedule posted in the shed. Mix dosage as instructed on packet. Confirm with supervisor before giving any new medication.
Afternoon block · 3:00 PM – 6:00 PM IST
3:00 PM
Second feeding round
Afternoon feed at standard quantity. Confirm water is still flowing in all drinkers.
Record: kg given, any issues with feeders
3:30 PM
Second egg collection & daily total
Count and grade as per morning. Add to morning count for the day's total. Store properly in trays, flat surface, cool area.
Record: PM count + combined day total
4:30 PM
Shed and walkway cleaning
Remove droppings from walkways and high-density feeding areas. Keeps disease pressure low and tracks clean.
5:00 PM
Bird health walkthrough
Walk through all sheds slowly. Signs to flag immediately: lethargy, discharge from eyes/nostrils, hunched posture, unusual sounds, not eating.
5:30 PM
Send evening WhatsApp report to Roshan
Use evening template: day total eggs, feed total, orders dispatched, revenue collected, any issues. Send by 5:30 PM IST.
Evening close · 6:00 PM – 6:30 PM IST
6:00 PM
Secure all shed doors
Latch and verify all doors. Predators (mongoose, wild dogs) are most active at night — this step is not optional.
6:10 PM
Water tanks topped up for overnight
Birds drink significantly overnight and early morning. Full tanks = healthy birds the next day.
6:15 PM
Update daily logbook
Enter all figures: AM eggs, PM eggs, daily total, grade A/B/C breakdown, total feed used, deaths, weather, any notes. This is Roshan's audit trail.
6:30 PM
Lock premises and sign off
Supervisor locks main gate and confirms to Roshan via WhatsApp if any issue arose during close.

escalation protocol
Critical — call Roshan immediately, any time of day
10+ bird deaths in one day · visible disease spreading through flock · theft, fire, or flood · complete water or power failure with no resolution path
Important — WhatsApp Roshan within 2 hours
Laying rate below 75% for 3 consecutive days · customer dispute unresolved · feed stock drops below 7 days · equipment failure affecting operations · worker absent with no cover · supplier price increase > 10%
Routine — include in daily or weekly report only
Single bird deaths (normal attrition) · minor repairs completed under ₹500 · new retail customer acquired · minor delivery delay resolved · normal supplier communication
